Early Tech Years

I attended The Ohio State University for Computer Science and Engineering. Through the time I spent there, I completed projects in C++, Java, C, Assembly, and Ruby on Rails. The most memorable project I worked on for curriculum would be the Assembly methods to handle strings, which gave you appreciation for even few built-in C methods. I had also joined up with a small entreprenuer group, who had the idea to install webcams in popular bars & venues around downtown Columbus. I was responsible for taking the video feed and creating the app layout & design, along with the database work of storing the venue hours and specials.

I had to leave school for financial reasons, but wanted to put what skills I had to use. I started making websites for small businesses, using Ruby on Rails, which I had grown to love. My favoite project I worked on was a webapp called "RecWorkouts"- in the requirements defined a "workout plan", "exercise list", and "sets", of which recursively allowed the program to walk the user through a complete workout. I was responsible for creating the databases & data structures, as well as website development & design.

Early Adult Years

I had 3 small contracts and site, but after, unfortunetaly, business died out. Admittedly, I was self taught for most web technologies, and was not equipped or prepared to deal with the marketing & business aspects as well. I spent 5 years working in fast food, developing a work ethic, eventually getting me promotions to management. I took an opportunity as a landscaper 3 years ago, and was promoted to crew leader within the first 3 months.
